
\section{On Inner Mission}

"Folkebladet," November 22, 1893.

Our point of departure is this: that Inner Mission is simply the proclamation of the Word and the administration of the Sacraments for the founding and upbuilding of congregations in such places where the people are either too poor or too indifferent themselves to sustain the work of the church. We do not regard mission work essentially as an extension of the church body, but as a new formation of Lutheran congregations. We do not regard the mission contributions as ordinary general expenses of the church body, for which reason they do not pass out of the church treasury either. We do not regard the mission gifts as an instrument of power in the hand of an accidental majority, so that it may employ these holy gifts according to a whim of the majority. We do not regard the mission committee either as a part of the governing board of the church body or as an independent mission board, but as a serving intermediary between the congregations that give and the congregations that receive help. We therefore believe that fidelity to the church body in mission matters is the greatest possible zeal for the salvation of souls, for the upbuilding of Lutheran congregations, for the diligent proclamation of the Word, and for the proper use of the Sacraments.

It could therefore very well be that there would be great fidelity in mission work even if we should help a congregation of Hauge’s Synod or of the Norwegian Synod in a place where it was truly needed. The more genuine Lutheran impartiality can be shown in Inner Mission, the better for our people, for our Church, and for the Association. . . .

The minority’s mission committee asks no other question of the one it supports than this: Is there need, and has help been denied by the majority? Whether the congregations supported by us will promise help to Augsburg or not, we have not asked them about that. We know that they would suffer serious loss in the proclamation of the Word if we did not help them; therefore we help, as far as we can.

Georg Sverdrup.
